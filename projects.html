CampusPath

  • Status: Complete

  • Languages: Java, HTML, CSS, JavaScript

CampusPath was a Google Maps like web application designed for the University of Texas at Tyler main campus. The idea was for users to be able to enter in a destination anywhere on campus and be directed to said destination using the paths and sidewalks throughout the campus.

CampusPath was developed in a group of 5 people. My role in CampusPath was primarily as a backend developer. Here I implemented features such as a Coordinate class and nearest neighbhor search. I would also assist in the implemtation of the database and the A* algorithm for shortest pathfinding. On top of this backend work I did assist in the constuction of some frontend elements such as the main menu and the map screen.

Budapest is the capital city of Hungary. It is best known for its arts and culture. It is a relatively small city, however there are much to see and do. Situated on thermal springs, there are many naturally heated baths to relax in, the Széchenyi baths are the largest with 15 indoor baths and 3 outdoor. There are many spectacular viewpoints in Budapest, great for capturing the views of the city. From 360 panoramic views up at St Stephens Basilica to a wide view of the parliament and the River at Fisherman’s Bastion. Visit the Museum of Fine Arts and enjoy a day amongst famous European art. Classical music lovers will appreciate a performance at the Academy of Music.
